"Horizontal Gene Transfer" by Rona Rangsch

"Share DNA, share love" contest Winner


The Endless Wanderer is proud to introduce you Rona Rangsch; the winner of the contest "Share DNA, share love" with her work "Horizontal Gene Transfer". A smart and mesmerizing visual representation of the HGT which do not fail to bring to the viewer sensations from the infinitely small to the infinitely large.


"Horizontal Gene Transfer" consists of taking the superpostion and multiplication of the images of different single-cell organisms (diatoms) as a visual equivalent of the process of horizontal gene transfer between them, the work shows a fictional kind of (diatom) protocell.


Rona Rangsch is a German physicist and multi-media artist. She has started her art career in the 2000s while being a guest student at the Dusseldorf's Academy of Art. Before that, she has graduated in Physics from the Universities of Saarbrücken and Cologne. She's a member of the German Physical Society since 1995 and the German Artists' Association since 2013.


The project 'Nature happens' is based on Rangsch's fascination of both the diversity and complexity of living nature and the boldness of scientists who methodologically address the multi-layered mechanisms underlying the development of life. She believes that the insights gained by science do not lessen the magic inherent in nature.


Discover more about her on her website http://www.rangsch.de/



"The Cosmic Eyes" by Maria Marinho Edgell

"Share DNA, share love" contest Second place


The Second place goes to Maria Marinho Edgell with the work "The Cosmic Eyes". A terrifying, therefore fascinating and strong work transporting whoever watch it, in the deepness of an endless maze.


The eye represents the evolution and the beginning of all things; a supernova, a big bang, scientific curiosity…. The eye is one of the evolution great achievements. The human eye begins with a light spot and gives the ability to look for prey. Our eye, an essential tool for survival, evolved for 500 millions years. Today, with scientific curiosity, observing our origins; the stars and genetics, gives us a cosmic awareness that undermines the whole concept that humanity is composed of “races”, proven that by tracing human ancestry, as it is described on DNA.


“The cosmic eyes” is part of the project "Topography of body" about the human vital organs, using cutout on paper, adding layer by layer like a topographic map, representing the human evolution.


Maria Edgell is a Portuguese artist living and working in Porto.

Contact her on her Facebook account.


"Moments in November" by Tree Stokes

"Share DNA, share love" contest Third place


Tree Stokes comes in third place with "Moments in November", a colourful chaos-pretend work reminding of the ambassadors of this contest and (no longer) HGT, tardigrades and their wandering about.


Based in Los Angeles, Tree Stokes is an artist with a taste for watercolour which she used as an expression of her space-time revelation among Roman ruins. However, she also searched to explain the phenomenon through physcics, astrology, comparative mythology...


This series of paintings is a distillation of an awareness I experienced during my years working with transparent watercolor. I primarily painted direct marks of puddled color. As I painted, I felt that each slow-drying puddle held its moment. The final dried mark expressed its moment in the characteristics of the pigments used and the initial flow and direction of the brush mark. The brush marks themselves mesmerized me as I watched them dry, like time locked in amber. In this series of paintings, the brush marks themselves take precedence and build their own image, a visual record and expression of subjective moments. They are the color of moments. Each brush mark occupies its own space in time, in harmony with the others. The marks sometimes collect as fractals. The completed image they become is their shared space in time within the boundaries of a painting.
