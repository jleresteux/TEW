Did we, ever, observe a white hole?


By know, we have all heard about black holes. They have been observed and are now being analyzed, if not attempted to be recreated. Our very own galaxy hold one black hole in its center and many other galaxies, too.


However, white holes are much more rare, in fact, we may have found one in 2006 but we are not sure.


Theoretically, white holes are the time-reversed equivalent to black holes, and it is mathematically possible. According to General Relativity, past, present and future don't exist; time can go forward as well as backward so the existence of white holes are completely right, in theory.


Yet, astronomical bodies projecting matter in space are not common and that is because of the second law of physics telling that the entropy always increases. Entropy refers to as the chaos, if a vase falls down and breaks, you are now in possession of a mess of a vase, that is entropy. Now, a black hole literally shreds objects in pieces thus complying to this law. The time-reversed action of a black hole, i.e. a white hole would create an object from shred of it. If we come back to the example of our vase, can you create the exact same vase out of shreds of clay? It is impossible to our current understanding of physics.


However, in 2006, astronomists recorded a long burst of gamma-ray that lasted 102 seconds, labeled GRB 060614 (for Gamma Ray Burst 2006 June 14). Nothing unusual there as long gamma-rays bursts, the most powerful burst in the universe, have been often recorded around supernovae and massive star formations. But, in the case of GRB 060614, none of these conditions were present, it just seems as this burst appeared from nowhere and it is definitely not clear what triggered such a long burst. CalTech astrophysicist Gehrels said: "This is brand new territory; we have no theories to guide us."


So...could it be a white hole? Alon Retter and Shlomo Heller suggested it in a 2011 paper, as GRB060614 doesn't fit as a usual GRB. They propose that each GRB not fitting the current established theory are white holes and propose that, unlike black holes, white holes are spontaneous and don't “work” long over time, explaining why space is not filled with “matter/energy-dispenser”. For Retter and Heller, white holes can appear anywhere and are not even, really, disturbed by the environment they appear in. They would, simply, come, throw out matter and energy and disappear.


All of it is pure theory and suppositions, the way black holes used to be. More observations of unfitting GRBs will be necessary to come to an understanding and then, we could discuss worm holes and space travel! One thing at a time!
