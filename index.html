To develop a complete mind: study the science of art; study the art of science. - Leonardo da Vinci

The Endless Wanderer gathers artists and scientists into this world of knowledge and aesthetic.

As an undivided mind The Endless Wanderer collects and binds the logical and creative spirits to work together, to answer and ask mysteries and to discover.

This collection wants the artists to express their impressions or feelings or views of any scientific phenomenon or personality as well as scientists to be inspired to unravel the mysteries pointed out by a drawing, a painting, a picture or a sculpture. To do so, and to encourage creation, The Endless Wanderer organizes juried and priced contests.

Each 3 months, we hold a contest which has a specified theme and/or media. All our contests are judged by experts in their respectful field (scientific or artistic or both).
Prices are given for the found best pieces in terms chosen by the jury (quality, accordance with the theme, creativityâ€¦). Winners will have his/her art work placed in our Permanent Exhibition as well as will be advertised on the most active art and scientific platforms of the Internet. A financial prize is also given.

The entry fee is fixed at 20Euros per submission. The fee is used as administrative purpose.

2015.12.01

Welcome!!

The Endless Wanderer sets for his long journey through knwowledge and aesthetic! On his way, he will collect geniuses and magicians to imagine, create and, surely, discover the inner beauty and structure of the world.

Each 3 months, The Endless Wanderer will organise a priced, juried and themed art contest to encourage the creation. Because our world is beautiful but, yet, uncomprehensible to all, themes of contests are about its secrets. But also, about the discoveries and those who discovered them, i.e. scientists of all times and subjects. The Endless Wanderer aims for artists, and everyone else, to comprehend the slightiest phenomenon of nature. As we, we can't always understand scientific results and analysis, therefore with a brief explanation, artists can create their own understanding of them. Or even, turn them around to produce new discoveries.

As example, Maurits Cornelis Escher has been inspired by the "Impossible stairs" from Lionel and Roger Penrose who had been inspired by Escher's work themselves. A collaboration between artist and scientists which led to a new aesthetic, a wonderful art work and better understanding of optical illusions.

The Endless Wanderer will install a public place, a forum, for scientists and artists to discuss and share ideas. But, meanwhile, the Facebook and Twitter @TEW2121 accounts can be used for this purpose.

To artists, please read our contests themes, create and submit your artwork.
To scientists, please continue to discover, observe our permanent exhibition to get inspired in researches and share your thoughts and ideas that you'd want to be represented.

2016 Spring Contest

Theme: White holes, black holes and wormholes

Celebrating the 100 years of Ludwig Flamm examination of Schwarzschildâ€™s solution (black holes) to Einsteinâ€™s general relativity field equations, leading to the theory of whiteholes.

A white hole is the theoritical time reversal of a black hole. While a black hole "sucks up" matter, a white hole would eject it. Flamm also considered the mathematical possibility of a connection between "entrance" black hole and "exit" white hole, i.e. a wormhole which could connect two different parts of the same universe or of different universes. Later, Einstein and Rosen explored it further to what we know now as the Einstein-Rosen bridge.

While black holes and wormholes are usually represented in art or fiction, white holes on their own are rarely pictured. The theme of this contest is focused on white holes but black holes and wormholes will be accepted too.
